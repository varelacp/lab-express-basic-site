About Studio Ghibli

Studio Ghibli is a Japanese animation film studio founded in 1985 by directors Hayao Miyazaki, Isao Takahata, and producer Toshio Suzuki. The studio is known for its visually stunning and emotionally resonant films, which have captivated audiences around the world.

About Hayao Miyazaki

Hayao Miyazaki is a Japanese film director, producer, screenwriter, animator, author, and manga artist. He is the co-founder of Studio Ghibli and has directed many of the studio's most popular films, including "Spirited Away," "Princess Mononoke," and "My Neighbor Totoro." Miyazaki's films often explore themes of environmentalism, pacifism, and the relationship between humans and nature.
